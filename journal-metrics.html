What these metrics are — and aren't

  • 2-yr mean citedness is OpenAlex's open equivalent of an impact factor: average citations this year to papers from the previous two. It is not the official Journal Impact Factor (Clarivate) or SJR (Scimago) — those are proprietary and can differ; we link each journal's Scimago page so you can check.
  • h-index here is the journal's, computed over its whole history — older journals score higher by longevity alone (Hirsch, 2005).
  • Impact ≠ fit. Editors desk-reject good papers sent to the wrong journal. Weigh the "works on your topic" column — evidence the journal actually publishes your conversation — at least as heavily as any metric.
  • Beware predatory journals. An APC alone is normal (many legitimate OA journals charge one); pressure tactics, fake metrics, and no real peer review are not. DOAJ listing is a good sign for OA journals. Checklist: Think. Check. Submit.
  • Reference: Hirsch, J. E. (2005). An index to quantify an individual's scientific research output. PNAS, 102(46), 16569–16572. https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.0507655102 · Priem, J., Piwowar, H., & Orr, R. (2022). OpenAlex. arXiv. https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2205.01833
