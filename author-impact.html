Method & honesty

How these numbers are computed — and their limits

  • Fractional works = Σ (1 / number of authors) across a researcher's papers — credit shared with co-authors, the fairer denominator for comparing solo vs large-team authors.
  • Weighted citations / paper = mean of (citations ÷ number of authors) per paper — citation credit split the same way.
  • Author position & corresponding come from OpenAlex authorship order and its corresponding-author flag; both can be imperfect for older or non-English records.
  • Retraction signals use OpenAlex's is_retracted flag. A flag is a prompt to look, not a verdict — always read the notice itself. Deeper integrity screens (Problematic Paper Screener, Scopus-discontinued lists) need external sources and aren't computed here.
  • Coverage is OpenAlex's (240M+ works): strongest for recent, English, DOI-bearing output. Name disambiguation can split or merge people — that's what the merge tool is for. We fetch up to 300 works per profile.
  • Data: Priem, J., Piwowar, H., & Orr, R. (2022). OpenAlex. arXiv. 2205.01833. An independent dashboard; not affiliated with any other author-metrics site.
