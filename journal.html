Aims & scope

Rigorous, useful, multidisciplinary business scholarship

JBMS publishes original, methodologically sound research that advances theory and practice across the management disciplines and their intersection with society and the natural environment. We welcome quantitative, qualitative, mixed-methods, and rigorous conceptual work, and we especially value research that speaks across disciplinary boundaries — for example, how entrepreneurship shapes sustainable development, or how digital transformation reshapes organizations. Our editorial standards are inspired by leading outlets such as the Journal of Business Research, Journal of Business Venturing, Business Strategy and the Environment, and Journal of Cleaner Production.

How review works

Every submission is read by humans — twice over

AI tools may assist authors and editors, but no decision is made by a machine. A handling editor screens each paper, at least two independent experts review it double-anonymously, and a human editor weighs their reports to reach a decision.

1SubmissionManuscript + declarations. Integrity self-checks required.
2Desk screeningA handling editor checks scope, quality, and ethics. Out-of-scope papers are returned quickly.
3Double-anonymous review≥2 independent reviewers score originality, contribution, methodology, clarity, literature, and ethics.
4Editor decisionAccept · minor revision · major revision · reject — with the reviewers' reasoning.
5Revision & productionAuthors respond point-by-point; accepted papers are copy-edited and published.
6PublicationOpen access in the monthly issue under a CC-BY licence.

Target first decision: 6–8 weeks. Before submitting, authors must run our Originality & AI Checker and Citation Integrity Checker — links are built into the submission form.

Publication ethics

Our standards, in the open

Integrity & misconduct. We follow the COPE Core Practices. Plagiarism, data fabrication or falsification, image manipulation, undisclosed conflicts, redundant or concurrent submission, and authorship abuse are grounds for rejection or retraction at any stage.

Authorship & contributions. All listed authors must have made a substantive contribution and approved the submission; we use the CRediT taxonomy. Ghost, gift, and guest authorship are not permitted.

AI use. Generative-AI assistance must be disclosed. AI cannot be an author. Authors are fully responsible for every claim, number, and citation — fabricated references are treated as research misconduct.

Corrections & retractions. We publish corrections, expressions of concern, and retractions following COPE guidance when the record needs it — transparently and promptly.

Appeals & complaints. Authors may appeal a decision once, in writing, with a reasoned rebuttal; a second editor reviews the case. Complaints about process or conduct go to the Editor-in-Chief.

Editorial independence & COI. Editors recuse themselves from papers where they have a conflict; reviewers must declare conflicts before reviewing.

Data & reproducibility. Authors are asked to state data availability and share materials where ethics permit.

Standards & inspirations: COPE · DOAJ transparency principles · ICMJE recommendations · CRediT · Think. Check. Submit.

Author guidelines

Preparing your submission

  • Original & exclusive. The work must be original, not published elsewhere, and not under review at another journal.
  • Paper types & length. Full research papers: 5,000–8,000 words. Case studies: 2,000–4,000 words. Word count includes references, tables, and appendices.
  • Abstract. Every submission must include an abstract of 200–300 words clearly outlining the purpose and objectives of the study, the research methodology, the key findings, and the practical and theoretical implications — followed immediately by 4–6 keywords.
  • Formatting & style. APA 7th Edition citation and formatting style · Times New Roman, 12-point · 1.5 line spacing · 1-inch margins on all sides · justified alignment.
  • Structure. Title, abstract, keywords, then the full manuscript (introduction, literature/theory, methods, results, discussion, conclusion, references). Conceptual and review papers may vary.
  • Anonymity. The manuscript text you submit should be free of author-identifying information — reviews are double-anonymous. Author details go in the separate authors panel.
  • References. APA 7th Edition; every in-text citation must appear in the reference list and vice versa (our Citation Integrity Checker verifies this).
  • Declarations. Ethics approval (where human/animal subjects are involved), conflicts of interest, funding, data availability, and AI use.

Open access & fees

Open access — free to publish for now

JBMS is fully open access under a CC-BY 4.0 licence — authors retain copyright, readers pay nothing. While we establish the journal, there is currently no submission fee and no Article Processing Charge — publishing with us is free.

Identifiers & indexing

DOIs and permanence

Every published article receives a persistent DOI (Digital Object Identifier) so it can always be cited and found. DOIs are minted through Crossref, the registration agency the scholarly world uses — as of 2026 its membership is a modest US$200/year for small publishers plus about US$1 per article, well within a journal funded by a transparent APC. Until Crossref registration completes, each accepted article carries a reserved DOI suffix (e.g. jbms.v1i7.0001) that becomes a live https://doi.org/… link the moment the prefix is activated — no article is left without a stable identifier.

A free bridge while membership is arranged: accepted articles can also be deposited to Zenodo, which mints a real DataCite DOI at no cost. Indexing (DOAJ, then Scopus) is pursued as the journal builds a publication record — we will never claim an index, metric, or DOI we do not actually hold.

JBMS is a new journal building its record issue by issue.
