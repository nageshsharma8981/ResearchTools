Where these ratings come from

  • FT50 — the 50 journals the Financial Times uses for its business-school research rank, © Financial Times. This tool carries the 2026 revision (which added Academy of Management Annals, American Sociological Review, and Psychological Science, and removed Human Relations, Journal of Business Ethics, and Organization Studies). Membership is binary and unusually consequential for business-school hiring. Verify at ft.com/ft50-journals.
  • ABS / AJG 2024 — the Academic Journal Guide, © Chartered Association of Business Schools. Ratings 4* (world elite) → 1. Verify at charteredabs.org.
  • ABDC 2025 — the Australian Business Deans Council Journal Quality List, © ABDC. Ratings A* → C. Verify at abdc.edu.au.
  • Scopus-based quartile, SJR, 2-yr citations/doc — from the SCImago Journal Rank 2025 release (the current edition), powered by Scopus data. Best quartile (Q1–Q4), SJR, H-index, and 2-year citations per document. Quartiles shift year to year — the comparison view links each journal's live Scimago page. Via scimagojr.com.
  • Impact (2-yr cites/doc) — citations per document over two years, the open analogue of an impact factor. It is not the official Journal Impact Factor (Clarivate JCR, proprietary). The comparison view adds a live OpenAlex 2-yr citedness for a current reading.
  • Rankings are opinions of their publishing bodies, they differ by design, and they are not quality guarantees for an individual paper. Always confirm on the official list your institution uses before submitting.
